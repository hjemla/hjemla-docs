# The Hjemla estimate

The Hjemla estimate is an automated estimate of what your home could be worth in today's market. It is calculated for every private home in Norway using advanced statistical and machine-learning models that analyse hundreds of data points per home.

## What affects the estimate

- **Home attributes** — size, location, number of bathrooms and bedrooms.
- **Market data** — asking price, description, comparable homes and days on market.
- **Historical data** — past sales and publicly available information.
- **Market trends** — seasonal changes in demand.
- **Owner information** — what you add about the home in "My home".

## Components of the estimate

The estimate is broken into three parts: **Historical market value**, **Attributes** and **Location**. This makes it easier to understand what drives the value.

## Estimate vs. e-takst

:::caution Important
The Hjemla estimate is not an e-takst. Banks usually require an e-takst from an agent or surveyor for a mortgage. Use the estimate as one of several starting points, and supplement it with e.g. a broker valuation and comparison with recently sold homes.
:::

## Accuracy

Accuracy depends on how much data we have about the home and the area. In areas with many sales the estimate is often more precise. Forced sales, inheritance and partial-ownership transfers are excluded so the estimate reflects ordinary market value.
