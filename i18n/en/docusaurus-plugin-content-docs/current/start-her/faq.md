# Frequently asked questions

## Does it cost anything to use Hjemla?
No. Hjemla is free for consumers. The service is made available in part through the partnership with If Forsikring.

## What is the Hjemla estimate?
An automated, data-driven estimate of what a home could be worth in today's market. It is calculated for every private home in Norway using statistical and machine-learning models.

## Is the Hjemla estimate the same as an e-takst?
No. The estimate is not an e-takst or a broker valuation. To take out or refinance a mortgage, the bank usually requires an e-takst from an agent or surveyor. Use the estimate as one of several starting points.

## Why does the estimate seem too high or too low?
Accuracy depends on how much data we have about the home and the area. A high estimate may be because we don't know about renovation needs; a low one may be due to unknown qualities such as a large roof terrace. The more unique the home, the harder it is to find comparables.

## How is the estimate calculated if no homes sold in my neighbourhood?
The model also draws on comparable homes outside your immediate neighbourhood, public data and market trends. In areas with few sales the uncertainty can be greater.

## Are forced sales and inheritance included?
No. The estimate is based on ordinary sales. Transactions that don't reflect real market price — forced sales, inheritance settlements or partial ownership transfers — are excluded.

## How do I correct information about my home?
Log in and go to "My home". There you can edit attributes, facilities and documentation. When the information is updated, the estimate is recalculated.

## Where does the data come from?
The foundation is public property data received via Ambita, enriched with data from listing platforms and brokerages, public registries, and information from home owners themselves. See [Data & sources](./data-og-kilder.md).
