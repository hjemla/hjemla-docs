# Glossary

Key terms you'll encounter on Hjemla, briefly explained.

**Hjemla estimate (Hjemlaestimatet)** — Automated, data-driven estimate of a home's market value.

**BRA — usable area** — The area within a home's enclosing walls; the most common area figure in listings.

**BTA — gross area** — Area measured to the outside of the external walls; larger than BRA.

**P-rom (primary rooms)** — Rooms for permanent use such as living room, bedrooms and kitchen.

**Asking price (prisantydning)** — The seller's expected price in a listing.

**e-takst** — Electronic valuation from an agent/surveyor that banks use for lending.

**Property transfer (eiendomsoverdragelse)** — A registered change of ownership for a property.

**Matrikkel** — Norway's official property register, using farm and holding numbers.

**gnr/bnr** — Unique identifier for a property in the matrikkel register.

**Registration (tinglysing)** — Public recording of ownership and encumbrances in the land register.

**Considered for sale (vurderes solgt)** — Feature where an owner non-bindingly marks a home as possibly for sale.

**Estimate components** — The estimate is broken into Historical market value, Attributes and Location.

**Average price (snittpris)** — Average home price in an area, used on neighbourhood pages.

**Complaint (reklamasjon)** — A claim about a defect in a home after purchase — often handled by a lawyer.
